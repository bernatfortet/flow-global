Named for the Grecian ideal of balance between a prevailing natural world and human endeavor, The Arcadium San Francisco is a consortium of Bay Area businesses, arts enterprises, non-profit organizations and human beings. We create gathering places to meet the needs for a healthy future.

Specifically, we make space to restore community, regenerate commerce, renew culture and embody care.

    Community

    Right now, we have the opportunity to strengthen our roots as a diverse community, one that is devoted to the commons and to learning, to accessibility, sustainability and intergenerational connection.

    Perhaps San Francisco and the whole Bay Area will lead the way globally, demonstrating how community might honor itself and emerge triumphant.

    “The Arcadium will infuse energy, magnetism, and cultural creativity into a jewel of the city. What’s striking to me is the project’s deep engagement with artists, entrepreneurs, culture-bearers, and local and national organizations. This commitment promises to enliven and connect each of the communities it will serve, in an ecosystem curated with impeccable care.”
    Nina Simons, Co-Founder of Bioneers
    & Founder of Everywoman’s Leadership

    Community engagement and participation is the bedrock principle of our endeavor. It is neither an obligation, nor a platitude. Instead it is our calling, privilege and joy.

    We will host participatory events to solicit feedback and guide vendor selection at a scale more often associated with social movements than real estate projects. Ongoing engagement with our communities of visitors and vendors will ensure that the variety and quality of our programming exceed expectations. And during our construction phase, a Direct Public Offering will provide opportunity for financial involvement at a low minimum investment threshold.

    Culture

    The Arcadium San Francisco will craft unique visitor experiences by spotlighting cultural offerings from San Francisco and the Bay Area. Performance and studio space, contemporary galleries, arts education and therapy, and large-scale installations will inform, inspire and engage.

    “ArtSpan is enthusiastic to contribute to the Arcadium SF’s vision, leveraging our 40-year history connecting the entire city of San Francisco to art and artists. We believe that engaging the public in the entire process of art making, from inception to installation on a finished professional gallery wall is the best way to inspire the importance of art and art ownership in our community.”
    Joen Madonna, Executive Director of ArtSpan

    Collaboration with cultural stewards will bring this program to life. With local community arts organizations and national arts foundations, we will offer residencies to Bay Area and international artists, with rotating exhibitions in permanent spaces and throughout the building. Contemporary galleries will set a stage for expanded discussion and commercial activity. The Arcadium is a place for the entire creative process -an immersive interplay between artists at work and people performing, learning, interacting, or buying.

    Stage and theatre space, as found at the Palace of Fine Arts, will be programmed with education, film, and performance institutions and community organizations. In public spaces, cultural heritage exhibits will set a discourse for the meeting place of past and future.

    Commerce

    We are forming a principled ecosystem of commercial and local service offerings including farm-to-table restaurants, fair trade boutiques, co-working space and other community oriented services designed to welcome, delight, nourish and sustain.

    “ At Kitchener Oakland, we’ve crafted community-oriented and affordable commercial kitchen space that supports micro and small food businesses with big potential. I'm excited to collaborate with the Arcadium San Francisco on a plant-based commissary kitchen paired with an open-layout teaching kitchen. This combination of commerce and education will be a hub for people to discover and share delicious food and promote environmental sustainability.”
    Sophia Chang, Owner/Founder of Kitchener Oakland

    Eateries are the literal and figurative hearth of the Arcadium SF, offering whole food plant-based cuisine with a commitment to accessibility, health, and environmental sustainability. The open-layout teaching and commissary kitchens make up a central hub, where small food entrepreneurs will provide food to be enjoyed on-site or taken home.

    Regenerative commerce prioritizes environmental sustainability and human wellbeing as a functional foundation for commercial enterprise. Our vendor ecosystem of will form around overarching goals of regenerating commerce, restoring community, renewing culture and embodying care. See page 27 of our Palace of Fine Arts Conceptual Proposal for selection criteria.

    Care

    In an interconnected world, caring for fellow humans, our environment, and our cultural history is integral to caring for our future. More than a virtue, we strive for embodied care through our work, our relationships and our principles. We believe Care is the strongest thread that links neighborhoods and human beings in the Bay Area. We care enough to have standards.

    At the Arcadium SF, we will tend to the body and the spirit with holistic wellness services and a new club that strengthens, builds and expands the acumen, power and positive impact of women.

    “This organization is what we all need! An incredible vision for ‘place and space’ to actively support women's growth, development, connections and, most importantly, ability to thrive. It’s inspired and practical—in equal measure. Improving the lives of women means improving the lives of all humans and the planet we share- a planet whose very survival is entrusted to us.”
    Kathy LeMay, Founder of Raising Change

    Connection promotes health, and facilitating shared participatory experiences over food, art, and social impact is the underlying ethic embodied in this building. We will partner with preeminent organizations that view health and healing from this perspective.

    A wellness pavilion will bring together integrative health clinics, nutritious food offerings, a therapeutic destination spa, and additional spaces for yoga, movement, fitness and dance.

    As an anchor tenant, Flow Global is a gathering place for women and their guests, and an educational institution focused on financial acumen, self development and social impact with workshops curated by a team of global women leaders in their fields.
